Cost of Living & Taxes

Housing prices, market rents (with suburb breakdowns), household income, state income tax, property tax, and sales tax rates. Expand Analysis & Context under each chart for per-city qualitative comparison.

Data Sources & Methodology

    Ongoing Expenses

    Recurring household costs that compound month over month regardless of whether you rent or own: utilities, auto insurance, and vehicle registration. These are separate from housing costs but often underestimated in relocation planning.

    Data Sources & Methodology

      Climate & Environment

      Temperature ranges, precipitation, snowfall, sunny days, and FEMA natural disaster risk scores (tornado, flood, wildfire). Weather and environmental risk are evaluated together as conditions that nature imposes on daily life.

      Data Sources & Methodology

        Lifestyle & Recreation

        Commute times (with suburb-to-city routes), public transit options, parkland access, crime rates, and qualitative lifestyle highlights: skiing, BLM/public land access, aviation connectivity, and motorcycle culture.

        Data Sources & Methodology

          Childcare & Education

          Annual center-based childcare costs by age group (infant, toddler, PreK), state public PreK enrollment rates, and school district letter grades with suburb-level breakdowns.

          Data Sources & Methodology

            Politics & Law

            Crime rates (violent & property), state employment law and worker protections, and firearms legislation. These factors differ dramatically by state and represent significant quality-of-life and legal compliance considerations for relocators.
